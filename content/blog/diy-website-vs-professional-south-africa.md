We're not going to tell you that DIY website builders are terrible. Wix, Squarespace, and WordPress.com have made it possible for anyone to put a site on the internet. That's genuinely valuable.

But "can I build a website myself?" is a different question from "should I build my business website myself?" The answer depends entirely on what that website needs to do for you.

Here's an honest comparison with real numbers.

## The DIY Route: True Costs

### Visible Costs
| Item | Cost |
|------|------|
| Wix/Squarespace plan | R150–R500/month |
| Custom domain | R100–R300/year |
| Premium template (optional) | R500–R2,000 once-off |
| Stock photos (optional) | R200–R1,000 |
| **Year 1 total** | **R2,500–R8,000** |

### Hidden Costs

**Your time:** The biggest hidden cost. Most business owners spend 20–40 hours building their first website on a DIY platform. If your time is worth R200–R500/hour (what you could bill or earn in that time), that's R4,000–R20,000 in opportunity cost.

**Learning curve:** Drag-and-drop builders have improved, but making a site look professional still requires understanding of layout, typography, colour theory, and user experience. "Good enough" often isn't good enough when your website represents your business.

**Ongoing maintenance:** Templates need updates. Plugins break. SSL certificates expire. Contact forms stop working. You become your own IT department.

**Speed penalty:** DIY builders add layers of code that you can't control. Most Wix sites score 20–40 on Google PageSpeed Insights mobile tests. That's not a minor issue — it directly affects your Google rankings and your visitor bounce rate.

### What DIY Gets Right
- Low upfront cost
- Full control over editing
- No dependency on a developer for text changes
- Good for simple informational sites

### What DIY Gets Wrong
- Speed (almost always slower than custom-built sites)
- Conversion optimisation (templates aren't designed for lead generation)
- SEO (limited control over technical SEO factors)
- Scalability (hard to add complex features later)
- Professional perception (template sites often look like template sites)

## The Decision Framework

### Choose DIY If:
- Your website is purely informational (no lead generation)
- You have more time than money
- You enjoy building things and have basic design sense
- Your business doesn't depend on website conversions
- You're testing a business idea before investing

### Choose Professional If:
- Your website needs to generate leads or sales
- You're running paid advertising (Meta, Google)
- Your time is more valuable than the cost difference
- You need the site to perform well on mobile in SA
- First impressions matter in your industry
- You want to focus on running your business, not your website
